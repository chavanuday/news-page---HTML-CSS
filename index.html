Raining Season has started

The Indian Meteorological Department has predicted more rainfall in the state as well as temporary traffic congestion, slippery roads, and water-logging. IMD also warned that several Odisha districts are likely to experience enhanced rainfall activities in the next four to five days, and a low-pressure area is expected to form over the same region during the next two to three days due to the formation of a cyclonic circulation over the northwest Bay of Bengal.

As no sign in rains n Mumbai yet

The much awaited rains have not yet arrived in the Mumbai city yet. With the delayed monsoon season, the city is grappling with a water shortage, the water stock in the city is now just 7.7 percent, a report by Times of India has stated. As of 20 June, the total water stock stood at 13.9 percent which is 1.59 lakh million litres and without the reserve stock, only 7.7 percent which is 1.11 lakh million litres is left.

Speaking of previous years' water cuts in the financial capital, in 2022, BMC had imposed 10 percent water cut in June and July as the water stock in the city reduced below 10 percent. Prior to that in 2020, 20 percent cuts were imposed in the month of August due to poor rains.
